Malappuram



Malappuram is a city in the Indian state of Kerala, spread over an area of 58.20 km² including it's outgrowths. The first municipality in the district formed in 1970, Malappuram serves as the administrative headquarters of Malappuram district. Malappuram consist of 3 natural divisions, lowland, midland and highland. The low land stretches along the sea coast, the midland in the centre and the highland region towards the East and North eastern parts. Malappuram is a northern district in Kerala known for its rich culture, historic significance and remarkable heritage. The region of Malappuram, speckled with small hills and mounds, justifies its name which in Malayalam means ‘Hill Top’.

Mini Ooty

Cheruppadi Mala and Mini Ooty viewpoints are two closely located picturesque sites of the Arimbra Hills, separated only by about 8 Km. Cheruppadi Mala view point is located on the north west whereas the Mini Ooty view point is along the south east. After about half an hour of travel from Karipur (Kozhikode Airport), via Puliyamparamba (around 11 Km in the South and South east direction), we reached this picturesque hairpin bend of Cheruppadi Mala. The small hill is popular for the striking beauty of foggy hills and the splendid views of the fresh water ponds, thus making this tourist attraction an eco-holiday destination of the District.

Nilambur Teak Museum

This museum nestles in the middle of Nilambur and once you get in, you forget that you are within a Town area! The museum hall could be improved a little, as the nuggets of info on Teak trees are scattered and only a discerning visitor could ingest the valuable and interesting data. Once outside themuseum, it's paradise on earth! The butterfly park is awesome and one feels so happy, comfy and high seeing these butterflies flitting around fearlessly. Overall, a nice place to visit.
